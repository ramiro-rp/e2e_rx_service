# Basic Functional Requirements (BFT)

## 1. Brief system description

**RSNA DICOM Service MVP** is an end-to-end service for chest X-ray pneumonia / lung opacity analysis. The system combines a training notebook, a FastAPI backend, a Streamlit demo client, Orthanc integration, and OHIF-based visualization.

## 2. Requirements applicable to the final service result

### 2.1. Study selection and intake

The service shall accept chest X-ray studies in DICOM format through local upload or Orthanc-based study selection. It may also accept PNG/JPG/JPEG files as a convenience mode, although the primary validated route remains the DICOM workflow.

- Study selection modes:
  - local manual upload;
  - selection of a listed study from Orthanc through the backend.

### 2.2. Minimum processing requirements

The backend shall apply an appropriate quality-control path according to the input type (DICOM or standard image).

- The backend shall return a structured processing status: `ACCEPT`, `ACCEPT_WITH_WARNINGS`, or `REJECT`.
- The service shall run calibrated inference and produce a structured response.
- The service shall not modify the original diagnostic image, its source metadata, or the accompanying original study information.

### 2.3. Service response content

The main MVP response is delivered as structured JSON.

At a minimum, it shall include:

- processing status;
- prediction label (`positive` / `negative`);
- calibrated probability and confidence-related fields;
- basic study metadata;
- URLs for generated visual artifacts;
- references to output artifacts (`run_id`, report file, derived DICOM when applicable).

### 2.4. Visual outputs and finding marking

For positive cases, the service may generate the original image, the model-input image, a heatmap, an overlay, and a transparent overlay layer for superposition.

- For negative cases, user-facing Grad-CAM artifacts shall not be exposed.
- Visual evidence shall be interpreted as an explanatory aid derived from model space, not as exact clinical segmentation.

### 2.5. Derived objects generated by the service

In DICOM workflows, the service may generate a derived Secondary Capture DICOM object and store it back in Orthanc.

- The original DICOM study shall not be altered.
- The derived object shall be marked as produced by the service in order to support traceability and prevent accidental reprocessing.
- DICOM SR is not implemented in the current MVP.

### 2.6. Integration and repositories

The backend shall communicate with Orthanc through its REST API.

- Orthanc acts as a PACS-like repository for source studies and derived objects.
- OHIF acts as the visualization layer for studies stored in Orthanc.
- The Streamlit frontend is a lightweight client for demonstrating inference and PACS ingestion; it does not own the core business logic.

### 2.7. Batch processing

The system shall support batch analysis of Orthanc studies that were not previously analyzed.

- The backend shall detect whether a study already contains an AI-derived series and skip it from the batch workflow.
- The batch response shall summarize the total number of studies, processed items, skipped items, failed items, and the count of positive / negative / rejected cases processed in that execution.

### 2.8. Restrictions

The following items from the broader institutional functional-requirement model do not apply to the current MVP:

- Kafka message bus;
- full clinical DICOM SR generation;
- advanced hospital synchronization across multiple enterprise systems;
- production physician worklist integration;
- broad coding annexes and institutional taxonomy appendices.

## 3. Documentation requirements

The service shall be accompanied by consistent documentation separated by layer:

- root repository README;
- global deployment guide;
- user manual;
- administrator manual;
- training and model documentation;
- final training notebook with exportable artifacts.
